## The Flow of Value (Or: Where It Breaks)

```
[ Member ] → works out at gym → data captured by sensors
    ↓
[ Gym/Platform ] → sells anonymized behavioral data
    ↓
[ Insurance Company ] → pays ¥50-250/person/year
    ↓
[ ? ] → profit
```

**The person who moved gets nothing.**

This is not a hypothetical. In China's physical fitness market, insurance companies already buy workout frequency, duration, and consistency data from gym chains and fitness platforms. They use it to:
- Adjust premium models
- Reward low-risk members
- Identify high-engagement cohorts

The data is valuable. The data generator is excluded.

---

## Why This Exists

The root cause is **Layer 2 — Verification**. Insurance companies cannot trust self-reported workout data. Gym platforms cannot prove that a specific person actually performed a specific rep at a specific time — not without building their own verification infrastructure.

So the market settles on:
- Intermediaries who aggregate noisy data
- Low-resolution signals (check-in counts, not rep verification)
- A closed loop where the member is the product, not the participant
