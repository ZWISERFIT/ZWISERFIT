# Why Multicoin's ILM Matrix Has a Missing Row — And We've Been Filling It for 7 Years

**By Suzanne Mok, Founder of ZWISERFIT**

In January 2026, Multicoin Capital published their Investment Logic Matrix (ILM) — a framework that classifies crypto investment theses along two orthogonal axes: the nature of the asset (Physical → Digital) and the nature of verification (Permissioned → Permissionless).

The resulting 2×2 matrix is elegant. Most investment narratives in crypto land neatly into one of three populated quadrants:

- **Physical + Permissioned** — supply chain track-and-trace, sensor networks that report to a single operator
- **Digital + Permissioned** — traditional SaaS, Apple Health, Strava — activity tracked on someone else's server
- **Digital + Permissionless** — on-chain reputation, decentralized identity, social graphs verified by consensus

And then there's the fourth quadrant:

**Physical + Permissionless.**

Multicoin noted this quadrant *should exist*. That someone should build a verification layer for **physical human behavior** that is as trustless and permissionless as Helium is for device location, or Hivemapper is for map data.

But they had no example to cite. No live case. Not yet.

---

## The Missing Quadrant Is Not Missing. It's In Dongguan.

For 7 years — since 2019 — a single gym in Dongguan, China has been running exactly this model.

ZWISERFIT started as one physical node: a facial-recognition turnstile at the entry of a 380m² fitness studio. Every entry is captured at 100% fidelity. Zero spoofing. 118 active paying members, each visit verified by hardware-level biometrics, bound to a DID, and recorded on a federated ledger.

This is not a proof of concept. It is 7 years of continuous production data — the longest running proof-of-physical-behavior pipeline in existence.

We didn't build this to fill a VC framework. We built it because the problem was obvious: the person who generates behavioral data gets nothing from it. Platforms capture it, package it, sell it. Zero consent. Zero ownership.

The only way to fix this is a verification layer that is:
- **Physical** — anchored to real human bodies, not digital proxies
- **Permissionless** — no single platform owns the data; the individual does, via DID

That is exactly the quadrant Multicoin described.

## What the ILM Missed — And Why It Matters

The ILM is correct in its structure. The gap it identified is real. But because no live example existed at publication time, the framework implicitly suggests that Physical + Permissionless is a *theoretical* quadrant — something for founders to go build.

The truth is it has already been built. You just weren't looking at a tiny gym in one of China's least penetrated fitness markets.

We deliberately chose Wanjiang, Dongguan — a district with the lowest fitness penetration rate in China — as our first node. If the model works here, it works anywhere. If behavior verification holds at 100% fidelity in a 380m² studio with 118 members, it scales to 380,000 members without changing the protocol architecture.

This is not a limitation. This is **the strongest signal you can get**: we proved it at the floor, not the ceiling.

## What ZWISERFIT Is — And What We Are Building

We are not a gym. We are not a fitness app. We are not another DePIN project.

**ZWISERFIT is the verification layer for human physical behavior.**

Just as TCP/IP became the fundamental protocol for data transmission — indifferent to what the data contains — PoPB (Proof of Physical Behavior) is becoming the fundamental protocol for verifying what human bodies actually do. The protocol is permissionless by design: any physical venue, any activity type, any geographical location can plug in.

Our architecture:

| Layer | What It Does | Status |
|:-----:|-------------|:------:|
| **Physical** | Turnstile + biometric sensors — anti-spoof hardware | ✅ 7 years live |
| **Data** | DID binding + MPC privacy — user owns their behavioral record | ✅ Live |
| **Protocol** | PoPB — verify human behavior on-chain | 🔄 WIP (aligning with a16z Arcade Token framework) |
| **Governance** | 9 AI Agents — 24/7 cross-validation audit by Stella | ✅ Live |
| **Value** | Behavior → asset — token warrants for access and rewards | 🔄 WIP |

AI Agents handle the repetitive, data-driven operations. Human partners deliver the warmth, the coaching, the recognition. Users own their data — the platform has no technical ability to access raw behavioral records.

**This is AI + Human co-symbiosis. Not automation for its own sake. Not human labor at scale. Each does what each does best.**
